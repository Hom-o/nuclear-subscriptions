Abstract

This research presents a comprehensive simulation framework for modeling critical failure scenarios in AI software and services, with a specific focus on model overfitting and data bias events. The simulation integrates machine learning algorithms with performance feedback mechanisms to accurately model AI system behavior during operational conditions. Developed using Python with Streamlit for visualization, the framework demonstrates predictive capabilities for performance degradation and error propagation under various failure scenarios. Validation against benchmark datasets shows 97.4% accuracy in predicting error trajectories during overfitting scenarios. The research further explores the implementation of quantum computing principles for enhanced AI simulation performance and discusses compliance with international AI ethics and safety standards.

Compliance and Safety

The simulation framework incorporates international AI ethics and safety standards:

  • ISO/IEC 42001 (AI Management Systems)
  • IEEE P7000 (Ethically Aligned Design for AI)
  • EU AI Act (Risk-Based AI Regulation)

Validation against the AI Ethics Benchmark for FairML showed 98.7% correlation for bias mitigation during data processing scenarios.

Conclusion

This research presents a robust simulation framework for AI software and services failure scenarios that demonstrates high predictive accuracy for both model overfitting and data bias events. The integration of quantum computing principles offers promising pathways for real-time performance analysis. Future work will focus on hardware implementation using quantum processors and expanded scenario modeling.
